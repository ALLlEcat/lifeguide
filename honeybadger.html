Honey Badger

Lifespan: 7-8 years in the wild, 24 years in captivity

Weight: Males 20-35 pounds, females 11-22 pounds

Diet: Insect larvae, scorpions, birds, lizards, beetles, small mammals, roots, fruit

Predators: Occasionally predated by leopards, lions, hyenas, and crocodiles

Range: Subsaharan Africa, Arabian penninsula, India

Conservation Status: Least concern>

Fun Fact: Honey badger skin is thick and loose, especially around their necks, making it hard to hold onto them for predators and protecting them from bites or porcupine quills. They are also resistant to both snake venom and bee stings.
