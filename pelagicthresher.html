Pelagic Thresher Shark

Lifespan: 15-20 years

Top speed: About 30 mph

Weight: 150-200 lbs

Diet: Small fish, including mackerel, herring, and small squid

Predators: Orcas and larger sharks

Range: Tropical and subtropical Indian and Pacific ocean

Conservation Status: Endangered

Fun Fact: Thresher sharks use the long upper lobe on their caudal fins to stun prey. They herd a school of fish into a ball and swim through it, creating shock waves with their tails that can stun fish.


Fun Fact: Pelagic thresher sharks can jump 10 feet (3 meters) out of the water. Why they do this is not completely understood, but it is believed to be related to hunting.
