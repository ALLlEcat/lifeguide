African Clawed Frog

Lifespan: 15-20 years

Top speed: 2.5-5 mph

Weight: 7 oz for females, 2 oz for males

Diet: Insects, small fish, worms, tadpoles

Predators: Birds, such as herons or egrets, as well as snakes and mammals

Range: Sub- Saharan Africa, invasive to other parts of the world, including the USA, Chile, France, and Indonesia

Conservation Status: Least concern

Fun Fact: The African clawed frog was the first vertebrate to be successfully cloned, 33 years before Dolly the sheep.


Fun Fact: African clawed frogs were used as pregnancy tests, known as the Hogben test, in the 1930s-1960s. A woman's urine was injected into a frog, and the frog would lay eggs within 24 hours if the woman was pregnant. This is because it responds to a hormone in the urine.
